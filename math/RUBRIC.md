# Mathematics Department: Grading Rubric & Assessment Standards

## Department Scope
This rubric applies **exclusively** to the Mathematics vertical. Other departments (Science, English, Social Studies) follow their own specialized assessment criteria as established by the Administrator.

## Core Philosophy
Our grading system is designed to reward foundational mastery and encourage intellectual risk-taking. We use a **Weighted Mastery** approach to distinguish between essential "building block" skills and advanced "genius" level synthesis.

## Weighted Scoring System

| Problem Tier | Weighting | Description |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Simple (Foundation)** | **2 Points** | Core concepts, procedural fluency, and direct application. Errors here indicate a need for remedial focus. |
| **Complex (Advanced/Genius)** | **1 Point** | Multi-step logic, non-linear reasoning, and "challenge" problems. These foster critical thinking with lower risk to the overall grade. |

## Grading Scale
| Percentage | Grade | Standing |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| 95% - 100% | **A+ / Mastery** | Ready for advanced extension / "Deep Dive." |
| 90% - 94% | **A / Proficient** | Solid understanding; minor "Double Down" focus needed on specific errors. |
| 80% - 89% | **B / Developing** | Requires targeted review of foundational "Simple" concepts. |
| Below 80% | **C / Needs Support** | Immediate instructional pivot and reassessment required. |

## Partial Credit Policy
*   **Simple Problems:** No partial credit. Foundation must be 100% accurate.
*   **Complex Problems:** Up to 85% partial credit for correct logical setup even if the final calculation is incorrect.
