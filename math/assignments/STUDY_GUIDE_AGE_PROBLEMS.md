# Math Mini-Lesson: The Time Machine Rule 🕰️

### The Problem
When we solve age problems that happen in the future or the past, it's easy to forget that **time happens to everyone.**

**Adhvish's common pitfall:**
> "In 20 years, Dad will be twice as old as his daughter."
> ❌ **Incorrect setup:** $D + 20 = 2 \times Daughter$

### Why is this wrong?
In this equation, only the Dad traveled 20 years into the future. The daughter stayed in the present! This would mean the Dad aged 20 years, but the daughter stayed the exact same age. That’s impossible!

### The "Time Machine" Rule
Whenever a problem says "In $X$ years" or "$X$ years ago," you must put **everyone** in the problem into the Time Machine by using **parentheses**.

**✅ The Correct Setup:**
1. **Step 1:** Put Dad in the machine: $(D + 20)$
2. **Step 2:** Put Daughter in the machine: $(Daughter + 20)$
3. **Step 3:** Now, apply the "twice as old" multiplier to the *future* version of the daughter.
> **Equation:** $(D + 20) = 2 \times (Daughter + 20)$

### Practice Challenge:
If a mother is 30 years older than her son, and in 5 years she will be 4 times as old as him...
1. **Present ages:** $M$ and $S$
2. **The Time Machine (+5):** $(M + 5)$ and $(S + 5)$
3. **The Equation:** $(M + 5) = 4(S + 5)$
