### **Problem 1: The Constant Difference (Simple - 2 pts)**
A father is currently 24 years older than his son. In 8 years, the father’s age will be exactly three times the son’s age. 

1. **Define Variables:** Let $F$ = Father's present age and $S$ = Son's present age.
2. **The Equation:** $F = S + 24$
3. **The Time Machine (+8):** Write the equation that represents them in 8 years.
4. **Solve:** What are their current ages?

**Vertical Specialization (Math Logic):** 
In mathematics, we call the age difference a "Constant." No matter how many years pass, $F - S$ will always equal 24. This is a powerful way to check your work!

### **Problem 2: The Double Jump (Complex/Genius - 1 pt)**
Four years ago, an aunt was 5 times as old as her nephew. In 6 years, she will be only twice as old as him.

1. **Define Variables:** Let $A$ = Aunt's present age and $N$ = Nephew's present age.
2. **The Past Machine (-4):** Write the equation for 4 years ago.
3. **The Future Machine (+6):** Write the equation for 6 years from now.
4. **Solve:** What are their current ages?

**Vertical Specialization (Systems Thinking):** 
This problem requires a "System of Equations." You have two unknowns ($A$ and $N$) and two distinct points in time. By anchoring both equations to the **Present**, you create a bridge between the past and the future.
